⓵ latent , but capable of awareness, which Freud calls the preconscious;

The structure of personality according to Freud

♔ID
●The very concept of "Id" originates in Latin ("id" means "it"). According to Freud's ideas, Id implies exclusively innate, instinctive, primitive aspects of a person's personality. The area of its functioning is the unconscious.
●Id represents the initial structure of the human psyche and serves as the primary principle of a person's entire life – an immediate outburst of psychic energy, which is produced through biologically conditioned impulses, among which aggressive and sexual predominate. This immediate outburst is called the pleasure principle.

♙Ego
The term "Ego" comes from the Latin word "ego", which means "I". The ego is a component of the mental apparatus, responsible for human decision-making. The ego strives for the expression and satisfaction of the desires of the Id, but already in accordance with the restrictions imposed by the outside world.

♕Super Ego
For effective functioning in society, a person needs to have a system of values, ethics and norms that will be compatible with the system adopted in the society around him. And all this can be acquired in the process of socialization – to put it in the terminology of psychoanalysis, this is due to the formation of the Super-Ego, which also comes from the Latin language, in which "super" means "over", and "ego", as it was said, "I". Sigmund Freud divided the Super-Ego into two separate subsystems - the Conscience and the Ego-ideal.
● According to psychoanalysis, the entire conscious life a person's Ego is looking for compromise solutions that can satisfy the Id and Super-Ego that are in constant confrontation with each other



The main mechanisms of psychological protection according to S.Freud

●Freud believed that people use various defense mechanisms to resolve conflict or ease anxiety.
●Let's look at some of them:
Sometimes described as "motivated forgetting," repression is the process of removing thoughts and feelings that cause suffering from awareness. As a result of the action of repression, individuals are not aware of their disturbing conflicts, and also do not remember traumatic past events. For example, a person suffering from horrific personal failures, due to repression, may become unable to talk about this difficult experience.
▶Projection.
As a defense mechanism, in its theoretical significance, projection follows displacement. It is a process by which an individual attributes own unacceptable thoughts, feelings and behavior to others people or environment. Thus, projection allows a person to lay the blame on someone or something for their shortcomings or blunders. A golfer criticizing his club after a bad shot demonstrates a primitive projection.

▶Substitution.
In a defense mechanism called substitution, the manifestation of an instinctive impulse is redirected from a more threatening object or person to a less threatening one. A common example is a child who, after his punished parents, pushes his younger sister, kicks her dog or breaks her toys. Substitution is also manifested in the increased sensitivity of adults to the slightest irritating moments.

▶Rationalization.
Another way for the ego to cope with frustration and anxiety is to distort reality and thus protect self-esteem. Rationalization has to do with false argumentation, thanks to which irrational behavior is presented in a way that looks quite reasonable and therefore justified in the eyes of others.
