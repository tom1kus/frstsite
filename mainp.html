Maslow 's Pyramid

Maslow assumed that human needs are distributed by levels - from the simplest to the highest. At the same time, the desire to satisfy the higher needs arises when we satisfy the lower ones.
Imagine that a person needs food and shelter. It is unlikely that he spends a lot of effort on developing some of his abilities. His thoughts and efforts are focused on eating and finding a place to live. But if a person has already provided himself with food and accommodation, then he has the motivation to create a family, to succeed at work. And when everything is there — a comfortable home, a decent job, a good family, recognition in society - a person can think about the realization of lofty needs. For example, he worked as an engineer all his life, but dreamed of learning to draw or play the guitar. And a person begins to close these sublime needs.


Temperament

■ Temperament is a set of formal and relatively stable characteristics of the behavior occurring in the energy level of behavior and in the timing of reactions (Miller S. N.)
■ The Creator of the doctrine of temperament is Hippocrates (5th century BC). He pointed out that people differ in the ratio of 4 main juice or body fluids – blood, bile, lymph, and phlegm. He believed that the optimal ratio of these juices determines the health of the body.

● Galen (2nd century AD), a disciple of Hippocrates, developed the first typology temperament. In the work "Temperament" (Latin temperamentum - proportionality, the right measure), he pointed out that a mixture of these liquids underlies 4 types of temperament: sanguine, choleric, melancholic and phlegmatic.
● Hippocrates compared a person's temperament with the peculiarity of his behavior, through the predominance of "fluid" (four elements) in the body.

⓵ Choleric. The body is dominated by yellow bile (hole, "bile), which makes a person impulsive "hot"
⓶ Phlegmatic. (phlegm, "sputum") lymph prevails in the body. Person it is characterized by its calmness and slowness.
⓷ Sanguine. (sanguis, "blood). Blood prevails. The person is more mobile and cheerful.
⓸ Melancholic. (melena and hole, "black bile"). The human body is dominated by black bile, which makes it sad and fearful.


But also do not forget that most people have a mixed temperament, it's all very individual!
