Psychodynamic direction in personality theory

If the individual unconscious and consciousness are purely personal lifetime acquisitions, then the collective unconscious is a kind of "memory of generations", that is, the psychological inheritance with which the child is born. Jung wrote that "the content of the collective unconscious is only minimally shaped by the personality and in its essence is not an individual acquisition at all. This unconscious is like the air that everyone breathes and that does not belong to anyone."


The collective unconscious

The collective unconscious is some mental patterns of behavior accumulated over millennia of the existence of a living being, which either a living being in general or a person encounters. *The collective unconscious is: ** the psychic memory of all mankind; •* psychic memory of all living things; •* some storage of archaic experiences accumulated during the existence of a living being on Earth

The content of the collective unconscious consists of archetypes- forms that organize and channel the psychological experience of an individual. Jung often referred to archetypes as primary images, as they are associated with mythical and fairy-tale themes.
He also believed that archetypes organize not only individual, but also collective fantasy (for example, they underlie the mythology of a people, its religion, defining the psychology of a people, its self-consciousness). Through the actualization of certain archetypes, culture also influences the formation of the individual human psyche.

∎The persona (personality) is the uppermost layer of the personal conscious; the Ego is its deeper layer. Below is the unconscious, first individual, then collective. The uppermost layer of the unconscious is the double of the Self, its shadow; the next layer is the soul (Anima and Animus); the lowest layer-objectively (self).

∎The archetypes of the Anime and the Animus are two components of one whole. They reflect Jung's assumption that each person carries certain psychological characteristics of the opposite sex.

∎The Shadow archetype is the center of the personal unconscious, the focus for the material that has been pushed out of consciousness; it is a kind of reverse, dark side of the Self.It is most deeply rooted in the animal past of man.Jung considered it a kind of legacy of lower forms of life.

∎The self is the archetype of the integrity of the personality. "The self," Jung writes, "means the whole personality. The whole personality of a person cannot be described, because his unconscious cannot be described." According to Jung, "the conscious and the unconscious are not necessarily opposed to each other, they complement each other with wholeness, which is self."

∎The ego is the center of consciousness and therefore plays a major role in our conscious life. The ego creates a sense of awareness and consistency of our thoughts and actions.At the same time, the Ego, being on the verge of the unconscious, is responsible for the connection (fusion) of the conscious and the unconscious. When the harmony of this connection is violated, neurosis occurs.


Formation of personality

Ⅰ) The first stage of individuation is the analysis of the person. The persona performs secret functions, but at the same time it is a mask hiding the self. The external manifestation of the personality (name, title, position, personal car, etc.) is not yet its essence. "Analyzing the persona," Jung points out– "we tear off the mask and discover that what was called individual is actually collective."
Ⅱ) The second stage is awareness of the shadow. If we recognize its reality, we can free ourselves from its influence. In addition, awareness of the personal unconscious is the way to eliminate neuroses.
Ⅲ) The third stage of individuation is a meeting with Anima and Animus. Jung himself referred to this archetype as a real being located in the recesses of his soul. He consulted with his Anima, considered her opinion, studied with her.
Ⅳ) The fourth stage is the analysis of the self. "The self is our life goal, because it is the most complete expression of that fateful combination that we call individuality." Jung believed that "a person should be himself, should carry his own individuality, that center of personality that is equally removed from the unconscious and from the unconscious; we should strive for this ideal center, to which nature seems to direct us."
